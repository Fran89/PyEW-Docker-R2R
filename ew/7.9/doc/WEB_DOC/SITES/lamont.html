Network Characteristics

The LCSN is operated by Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory in cooperation with the State University of New York at Potsdam, The University of Vermont, Middlebury College (Vt), Westchester Community College, Rensselaer Polytecnic Institute, the Adirondack Ecological Center, and the Delaware Geological Survey/University of Delaware. The network operation is supported by the National Earthquake Hazard Reduction Program (NEHRP) through the U. S. Geological Survey.

Network Configuration

  • 22 analog, short-period telemetry stations
  • 2 broadband stations with direct digital recording
  • 6 strong motion stations (5 with remote access)
  • 10 USNSN broadband stations in monitoring region, received via VSAT
  • Analog network data digitized by 4 Cutler/SUN systems, with data transfer via internet.
  • VDL operational, with Broadband station PAL.
  • 2 additional digital broadband stations to be installed in 1998.
  • Network covers an area 610 km from north to south and 220 km from east to west.
